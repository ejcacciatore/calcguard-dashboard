Addressing the Core Challenges of CAT

Transforming regulatory infrastructure through federated architecture principles

📄 Read the comprehensive analysis of CAT challenges
💰

Ballooning Costs & Unclear Budgeting

Shared Utility Model: A federated node architecture allows participants to share costs, distribute compute loads, and perform modular infrastructure upgrades.

📈

Uncontrolled Scope Creep

Controlled, Versioned Expansion: This model enforces strict, role-based data access with versioned APIs. New data fields must pass defined schemas, preventing uncontrolled add-ons.

🏛️

Lack of Industry Governance

Shared Oversight Protocol: A new governance model allows regulated participants and SROs to share oversight through transparent, auditable logs and stakeholder voting mechanisms.

🔍

Opaque Decision-Making

Radical Transparency: An open-source architecture with immutable audit logs and modular transparency layers provides clear visibility for the SEC, FINRA, and all participants.

💸

High TCO & Indirect Costs

Aligned Cost-to-Use: By enabling lightweight deployments for smaller broker-dealers and optimizing data handling, this approach significantly lowers the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO).

Encrypted Trade Lifecycle Graphs

Proposed architecture would replace raw logs with secure, context-rich graphs, enhancing data integrity and privacy while providing comprehensive order flow visibility.

Distributed Compliance Ledger

⚡
Real-Time Regulatory Access

Proposed infrastructure would enable millisecond-level regulatory queries while maintaining data sovereignty for market participants.

🛡️
Federated Security Model

Zero-trust architecture ensuring participants maintain complete ownership and control of their data while meeting all regulatory requirements.
