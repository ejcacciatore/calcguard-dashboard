Re-architecting CAT: From Centralized Cost Sink
to Federated Utility

Addressing the Core Challenges of CAT

Ballooning Costs & Unclear Budgeting

A federated node architecture allows participants to share costs, distribute compute loads, and perform modular infrastructure upgrades.

Uncontrolled Scope Creep

This model enforces strict, role-based data access with versioned APIs. New data fields must pass defined schemas, preventing uncontrolled add-ons.

Lack of Industry Governance

A new governance model allows regulated participants and SROs to share oversight through transparent, auditable logs and stakeholder voting mechanisms.

Opaque Decision-Making

An open-source architecture with immutable audit logs and modular transparency layers provides clear visibility for the SEC, FINRA, and all participants.

High TCO & Indirect Costs

By enabling lightweight deployments for smaller broker-dealers and optimizing data handling, this approach significantly lowers the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO).
